New Brunswick PNP

New Brunswick's Provincial Nominee Program uses an EOI-based system with dedicated streams for skilled workers, international graduates, and entrepreneurs.

Pathway Overview

The New Brunswick Provincial Nominee Program (NB PNP) operates an Expression of Interest (EOI) system. Candidates submit an EOI with their profile details, get scored based on factors like education, work experience, language ability, and connection to New Brunswick, and then receive invitations to apply through periodic draws.

NB PNP offers multiple streams targeting different applicant profiles, including Skilled Workers with Employer Support, Express Entry Labour Market, and the Strategic Initiative stream for Francophone workers. New Brunswick is actively recruiting healthcare workers, early childhood educators, IT professionals, and skilled tradespeople to fill critical labour market gaps.

New Brunswick offers a lower cost of living compared to major Canadian cities, a growing economy with opportunities across diverse sectors, and the unique advantage of being Canada's only officially bilingual province. It is an excellent choice for newcomers seeking a high quality of life with strong community support.

Eligibility Requirements

To qualify for the New Brunswick Provincial Nominee Program, you will generally need the following:

  • Valid Expression of Interest (EOI) submission through the NB PNP portal
  • Language proficiency of CLB 4 or higher (IELTS, CELPIP, or TEF)
  • Post-secondary education or equivalent foreign credential assessment
  • Connection to New Brunswick (job offer, family member, previous study or work experience in NB)
  • Age between 19 and 55 years at the time of application
  • Proof of settlement funds sufficient to support yourself and dependants in New Brunswick

Eligibility varies by stream. Contact us for a personalized assessment based on your specific situation.

Frequently Asked Questions

Submit an EOI with your profile details through the NB PNP online portal. New Brunswick scores and ranks candidates based on factors like education, work experience, language ability, age, and connection to the province. The highest-scoring profiles receive invitations to apply through periodic draws conducted by the province.

New Brunswick offers three main PNP streams: Skilled Workers with Employer Support (requires a valid NB job offer), Express Entry Labour Market stream (linked to the federal Express Entry system), and the Strategic Initiative stream (designed for Francophone workers and candidates with strong ties to New Brunswick).

It depends on the stream. The Skilled Workers with Employer Support stream requires a valid job offer from a New Brunswick employer. The Express Entry Labour Market stream does not require a job offer, but having one significantly strengthens your EOI score and improves your chances of receiving a draw invitation.

New Brunswick has strong demand for healthcare professionals (registered nurses, personal support workers), early childhood education (ECE) teachers, IT professionals (software developers, system administrators), skilled trades (welders, electricians, plumbers), truck drivers, and food service supervisors. These occupations frequently appear in NB PNP draw invitations.
