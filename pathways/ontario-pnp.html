Ontario PNP (OINP)

Ontario's Provincial Nominee Program offers multiple streams for skilled workers, international students, and employer-sponsored candidates targeting Canada's largest province.

Chat on WhatsApp Free Assessment

Pathway Overview

The Ontario Immigrant Nominee Program (OINP) provides a pathway to permanent residency for eligible workers who plan to live and work in Ontario. As Canada's most populous province and economic hub, Ontario offers the most job opportunities and the highest demand for skilled workers across virtually every industry.

A provincial nomination through OINP adds 600 CRS points to your Express Entry profile, which virtually guarantees you will receive an Invitation to Apply in the next Express Entry draw. This makes OINP one of the most powerful tools available for candidates whose base CRS score falls below the general draw cutoff.

OINP offers multiple streams designed for different candidate profiles, including the Human Capital Priorities stream, French-Speaking Skilled Worker stream, Skilled Trades stream, and various Employer Job Offer streams. Each stream has its own eligibility criteria, giving a wide range of candidates the opportunity to settle in Ontario.

Eligibility Requirements

Eligibility varies by OINP stream. General requirements for the most popular streams include:

  • Active Express Entry profile (required for Human Capital Priorities stream)
  • Occupation classified under NOC TEER 0, 1, 2, or 3
  • Job offer from an Ontario employer (required for Employer Job Offer streams)
  • Language proficiency of CLB 6–7 or higher depending on the stream
  • Educational credentials assessed through an ECA from a designated organization
  • Genuine intention to settle and work in Ontario

Stream-specific requirements differ significantly. Contact us for a personalized assessment to determine which OINP stream is the best fit for your profile.

Frequently Asked Questions

The Human Capital Priorities (HCP) stream targets Express Entry candidates with strong human capital factors including education, skilled work experience, and language proficiency. Ontario searches the Express Entry pool and sends Notifications of Interest to eligible profiles that meet the province's labour market needs. No job offer is required for this stream.

Once you receive a provincial nomination from Ontario, 600 additional points are added to your Comprehensive Ranking System (CRS) score in the Express Entry pool. This boost virtually guarantees that you will receive an Invitation to Apply (ITA) in the next Express Entry draw, regardless of your base CRS score. The nomination is reflected in your profile automatically after you accept it.

Not for all streams. The Human Capital Priorities stream and the French-Speaking Skilled Worker stream do not require a job offer from an Ontario employer. However, the Employer Job Offer streams (Foreign Worker, International Student, and In-Demand Skills) do require a valid job offer from an eligible Ontario employer. We assess your profile and recommend the most suitable stream.

The OINP typically takes 60–90 days to review and issue a nomination decision. After receiving your provincial nomination and the 600 CRS point boost, you will receive an ITA and then submit your federal PR application, which takes an additional 6–12 months to process. The total timeline from OINP application to PR approval is approximately 12–18 months.
